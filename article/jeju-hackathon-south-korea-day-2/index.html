In Day 2, the main focus was the idea development. After some discussion, we agreed to set our goal as an application to help promote small businesses at JeJu.

Step 1 - Idea Development

As our goal was finalized, we decided to choose a team name which can exactly reflect the goal. We independently wrote names and then tried to incorporate all key ideas from the names. Finally, we combined 2 key ideas: “blog” and “story telling” as our team and application name - Blogory

Luckily, we got a strong team. One team member from Thailand is a graphics design. He quickly drafted the logo and banner of our application:

The next step was the UI/UX workflow sketch. The organizers kindly provided us a A2 paper stand. Our team could freely draw and sketch our workflow:

My key contribution was to initiate the core idea during the on-the-ground research and slide preparation. With the help from the Thailand members, we came up with one slide to summarize the problem formulation after the on-the-group research

Next, we need to find some statistics to support our claims. For example, what is the percentage of small businesses in JeJu and South Korea? Luckily, the South Korea government published the figures every year. We found out that more than 97% people in JeJu/South Korea are working in small businesses (i.e. SMEs contributed 99% of workforce in JeJu and South).

Some team members were working on the prototyping. I kept working on the slides preparation. I prepared the final slide for the presentation. We would like to develop our application Blogory with the goal to break the language barrier by quality services offered by small businesses in JeJu/South Korea.

Step 2 - Mingling with other participants

Besides from development work, I also enjoyed talking with other people. I talked with a team of Vietnam students. I found out that he used a VERY special keyboard. He replied to me that the whole keyboard was made by him! (including the circuit board and the buttons). He brought the whole keyboard and travelled with him from Vietnam to South Korea.

Step 3 - Dinner time

Although the organizers provided us plenty of food, I decided to follow another team of Korean students to visit a local Korean restaurant. We chose a domestic Korean BBQ restaurant. Unlike Korean restaurant in Hong Kong, you MUST know how to cook by yourself. The waitress won’t offer help. They provided us a pair of scissors.

A native Korean participant helped most of the cooking work. Yet, I tried to do a small part by myself. It was not easy to cut meat using a pair of scissors

I recorded down the steps for Korean BBQ. First, the slices of raw meat were placed on the pan heated by charcoal. After that, the sides should be flipped. When both sides were almost ready, we needed to cut them into small pieces. At that point, we could add some Kimchi and vegetable to balance the favour of Korean BBQ

In traditional Korean restaurant, all meat is raw and won’t be seasoned. Thus, the restaurant provided us different kinds of sauce and some side dishes (e.g. different kinds of Kimchi) to enhance the favour of meat. The sauce was really nice!

We ended Day 2 with this wonderful meal. Day 3 will be the final demo day!
