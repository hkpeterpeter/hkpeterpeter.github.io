## Background

I am an experienced Hackathon organizer, participant, judge, mentor, 
and a HealthTech startup co-founder. I am now planning with a number of experienced 
hackathon organizers to re-invent tech recruiting via corporate hackathon. Feel free to drop me a message if you are interested.

The focus of this article is mainly for tech recruiting. If the main purpose 
is just for branding, sponsoring a hackathon should be the most cost-effective 
solution. There are many community/on-campus hackathon organizations 
who can reach out 100k+ developers in 100+ cities worldwide.

In the past years, I chatted with senior executives, recruitment managers, 
and developers working in technology companies, banks, and startups. 

The 2 most frequently asked questions are:

- Why is it so hard to find developers?
- Can sponsoring hackathon help me find good developers?

First, good software/hardware developers are abundant. The key problem is, 
except well-established companies, standard human recruitment activities
 (i.e. on-campus recruitment talks and booths, online job ads, etc.) 
 fail to capture their attention. Preparing well-crafted CV/resume 
 is **NOT** the required skill for good developers. Some excellent candidates may be excluded in the first stage. Even worse, a few rounds of programming tests / phone calls / face-to-face interviews are required to shortlist candidates from this biased pool. Companies may spend extra money to request services from tech recruiting firm, but the services they offered are mainly browsing GitHub/LinkedIn profiles and provides extra programming and written tests.

Unlike startup activities, you can always find enough good developers in hackathon. In a typical hackathon, developers contribute 60-80% of all registered participants. Now, move to the next question, can sponsoring hackathon help me find good developers? The answer is YES/NO.

## Why sponsoring hackathon is ineffective?

If you are an established gaming company and would like to hire an excellent fresh graduate to join your development team. You may decide to spend US$8,000 (an average monthly salary of a HR manager in United States) to sponsor an on-campus hackathon. That hackathon may attract 1000 participants (~200 teams, assuming 5 people per team). The optimal result is that you receive the top-3 teams from this hackathon. These 15 people should be outstanding enough and be interested in the gaming industry, but they may not all be good game developers (yet, it may be a bonus as you may be able to shortlist good candidates for marketing and designer team as well). In the worst case, all game developers in these winning teams are not interested to join your company. Your company may still gain some benefits (e.g. branding), but in term of tech recruitment, it will be a 100% lost.

Please note that, if the amount of participant is good enough (i.e. 100+), the number of participant does not really matter for tech recruiting (Yet, it matters for corporate branding). In other words, if the number of participant becomes 2000, your company may still only be able to receive the top-3 teams (i.e. around 15 top participants).

## Organizing a corporate hackathon

So, what is a better way to use hackathon as a mean of tech recruiting? You may consider to work with experienced people to organize a corporate hackathon. In an ideal case, the organizers and the corporate representatives should be within the same time-zone (and preferably, within the same city).

To effectively recruit tech talents, a corporate hackathon should be designed from scratch. The team should clearly define the goals. For example, if a corporation would like to promote the application programming interface (APIs) for the developers, the rules, regulations, and judging criteria of this hackathon should be tailor-made to maximise its usage. In this special case, that corporation should better exclusively brand the whole hackathon.

In some situations, it may be possible to inviting other corporations to co-host a corporate hackathon and share the expenses. For example, a commercial bank may decide to have digital innovation and transformation within their company. There is no specific APIs to be promoted. The bank may consider to partner with some other banks and tech companies (usually, vendors supporting some of the services) and proposes some current challenges for the corporate hackathon. In an ideal case, representatives from technology departments should be involved in formulating the judging criteria and be a judge as well. Although the winning teams may not be suitable for tech recruiting, the innovative ideas may be helpful for the digital innovation for the company.

## Conclusion

To conclude, there is no single copy-and-paste solution for tech recruiting via corporate hackathon. If you decide to do so, please consider to find experienced hackathon organizers from your city and start planning now.
