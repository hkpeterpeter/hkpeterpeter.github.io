A *standard* Hackathon is usually a community-driven competition without a specific goal. 
A data-centric Hackathon (Datathon), on the other hand, is mainly driven by industry with a goal 
to drive technical innovation and talent acquisition with a provided dataset and challenges faced 
by the real business. Rather than sponsoring community-driven Hackathon, 
I foresee that more and more business will take a lead to organize challenges/competitions, 
instead of treating Hackathon as a marketing and promotional event.

It is an honour to be selected to join AS Watson Hackathon 2018, 
although our team can't win any prize after nearly 36 hours of competition. 
A well-organized Hackathon should not be rated by the number of participants.
 AS Watson Hackathon is a good Hackathon, with the following observations in a participant's perspective:

- A good mix of quality data scientists, software developers, UI/UX designers, and even internal staff at AS Watson group. In addition, there is a pre-event screening process to invite the best participants to join this competition.
- Great team composition. For each team, it is mandatory to have at least one internal Watson staff member. Each team should also have at least one data scientist, one software developer, and one UI/UX designer.
- Senior management of AS Watson group is very supportive and have a forward looking mindset
- 10GB of real data is released for participants. It is still relatively small comparing with the daily transaction volume of their retail business, but it is enough for a 2-day competition
- Great number of technical mentor support (especially from Microsoft, nearly 20 technical mentors be present to provide support until 8-9pm in the first day of competition)

There are some minor issues to be improved:

- All participants experienced a slow WIFI connection problem in which most of us relied on our own wifi hotspot. It greatly affected the development work and also the final live demo presentation.
- Presentation rules are too strict. For example, only 1 person is allowed to present the idea and 1 person to control the live demo. The presenter is usually having a business user role, and s/he may not be able to clearly explain rationale in a data scientist perspective. It affects the presentation quality during the final demo session.

All in all, I sincerely wish that the senior management of the Watson group can continue supporting this type of Hackathon competition, and more and more business in Hong Kong will become open-minded to adopt Hackathon/Datathon to drive their internal technical innovation.
